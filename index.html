We are seeking ideas about how sexual health services for adults over the age of 45 can be improved in the UK.

Why sexual health for over 45s?

We all age. We cannot change that.

But understanding how to ensure access to essential sexual health services is something that we can change, and is, with your help, what we hope to change.

Sexual health is the ability for us to enjoy and express our sexuality free from physical, psychological, and social pressures. Sexual health is an integral part of our overall health and wellbeing.

People have needs and desires for intimacy and sex, regardless of their age. Issues such as low sexual function, distress about sex, or an elevated risk for sexually transmitted infections are common throughout every point in life. These are further complicated by disabilities and chronic conditions that are common among people over 45 years old.

Despite sexual health being an integral part of our overall health and wellbeing, sexual health services in the UK often focus on younger populations. The UK population is ageing, and the current NHS services are not up to the standard needed to support our ageing society.

Get Started

What do we ask from you?

If you have any ideas on ways to improve the sexual health services offered in the UK for over 45’s, we want to hear from you. We are interested to hear from people from all backgrounds so that submissions represent society at large - your voice matters to us.

Submission themes may include, but are not limited to, the following:

  • Ideas for reducing existing barriers that prevent older adults from accessing services

  • Ideas on how to improve the means through which sexual health services are delivered (Online/In-Person)

  • Ideas on how to encourage inclusive practices in the provision of sexual health services

  • Ideas on how to tailor sexual health services to the needs of older adults, including those with disabilities

  • Ideas on messages/campaigns to enhance perception and uptake of sexual health services in older adults

Guideline for Contributions

Eligibility (Participant)

  • Resident in the United Kingdom continuously in the last 6 months
  • All ages are welcome (participation of those aged 45 years and above is encouraged)
Eligibility (Submission)

  • Related to sexual health services in the UK
  • Relevant to adults age 45+
Language for entries

  • English

  • Innovation. Does the proposed solution promote new ideas towards addressing the issues described?

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an open call?

Open calls are ways in which the public is called upon to solve a problem via the solicitation of “ideas, reports or strategies.”

What is sexual health?

WHO’s working definition of sexual health indicates that sexual health is a state of physical, emotional, mental and social well-being in relation to sexuality; it is not merely the absence of disease, dysfunction or infirmity. Sexual health requires a positive and respectful approach to sexuality and sexual relationships (WHO, 2006).

Who can participate?

This call is open to anyone above the age of 45 years residing continuously in the United Kingdom in the last 6 months

Why should I submit?

Your submission will help to develop strategies with which we can optimise the provision of sexual health services for adults over the age of 45 and optimise the uptake of these services.

Is this call limited to a particular field in sexual health?

No. Submissions addressing any area of sexual health services and related experiences are welcome.

Can I make more than one submission?

Yes—you can submit as many ideas as you wish.

How will the submissions be assessed?

All submissions will be screened for eligibility based on the submission guidelines published. All eligible entries will then be reviewed further based on a pre-defined criterion and assigned a single score between 1 and 10. High scoring entries will be shortlisted and taken forward for analysis and dissemination

What should be the focus of my submission?

We welcome any ideas on practical strategies and ways that sexual health services can be optimised for over 45’s living in the UK. See the judging criteria

How can I send in a submission?

We accept many forms of submission. Please see the Contribution Format Options section for more information.

How can I encourage my peers to join?

You can also increase participation in this open call by sharing the details with colleagues and friends in-person and online using social media channels.
